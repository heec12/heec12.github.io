My name is Hee Choi, and I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at Georgia Tech in [Dr. Shi Joyce Sim's research group](https://simlab.eas.gatech.edu/).

I am a geodynamicist interested in early Earth. My research focuses on understanding how the first continents formed and how subduction operated in the early stages of Earth's history — problems that are especially compelling because so little geological evidence survives from that time. I started my research career asking whether continents could have triggered the onset of plate tectonics, and found that the answer is more complicated than that — which led me to the continental crust itself as the main object of study. I now use geodynamic models, two-phase flow simulations, and machine learning to investigate how ancient subduction zones produced the buoyant, felsic melts that built the earliest stable crust.

I am also interested in how advances in AI and computational tools can benefit the geoscience community — including developing methods for automated analysis of large-scale numerical simulations.

If you're here for the academic side of things, see my [CV](https://heec12.github.io/assets/data/hchoi_cv.pdf) or head over to the [Research page](https://heec12.github.io/research/).
